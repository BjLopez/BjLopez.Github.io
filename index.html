JoJo Rabbit, directed by Taika Waititi JoJo Rabbit is an anti-hate satire that takes place in World War II-era Germany. The story is about a boy named JoJo whose imaginary friend is Adolf Hitler and is a devout follower of him and even a member of the Hitler’s Youth. He comes home to discover a Jewish girl named Elsa whom his mother is hiding within the wall. JoJo’s mother wants him to enjoy his childhood rather than being obsessed with war. He familiarizes with Elsa and befriends her and, but Hitler attempts to push him away. The subject of the piece is JoJo breaking through a swastika and shattering it which symbolizes his development as a character. The palette of black, white, and red is taken from Nazi iconography and it is satirical in nature but tells an anti-hate narrative, so I decided to parody such a hateful symbolism.
